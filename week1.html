OJT WEEKLY BLOG

Week 1

February 23–27, 2026

I spent the final week of February focused on keeping our office running smoothly by making sure all important documents and requests were handled on time. My week started with the Monday morning flag ceremony and wrapped up with the flag lowering on Friday afternoon. Participating in these traditions always serves as a good reminder of why the work we do at the Department of Health matters for the public.

Most of my days were dedicated to managing the steady flow of paperwork and digital files. I took care of recording and routing key documents like contracts, Memorandums of Agreement (MOA), and various regional office orders to make sure they reached the right divisions without getting stuck. I also stayed on top of personnel matters by processing leave applications, Daily Time Records (DTR), and attendance reports, while ensuring that purchase requests and orders were moved along quickly so that our teams had the resources they needed.

To keep everything organized, I used the DTRAK system to log document movements and handled our office emails by forwarding them to the Record Section or the specific units that needed to take action. Beyond the administrative side, I also spent time assisting visitors and clients who came into the office, helping them with their concerns or directing them to the right person. By staying organized and keeping our communication lines open, I was able to help our office stay on track and maintain the steady pace of our daily operations.
